About Me

Hello and Welcome to My Homepage!

I am currently a Postdoctoral Research Fellow working with Prof. Cheng Li in the Department of Statistics and Data Science at the National University of Singapore. My current work focuses on Bayesian spatial and spatio-temporal modeling, with applications in various real-world problems.

I have successfully defended my PhD thesis, "Analyzing House Price Dynamics Using Spatiotemporal Models," in the Decision Sciences area at the Indian Institute of Management Bangalore. Under the supervision of Prof. Prof. Soudeep Deb, my research focused on developing and applying spatial and spatiotemporal models to understand housing market dynamics. Before joining IIM Bangalore, I worked as a Senior Data Analyst at Indxx, LLC. I hold a Master's degree in Mathematics from the Indian Institute of Technology Delhi, and a Bachelor's degree in Computer Engineering from the Indian Institute of Information Technology, Design and Manufacturing, Chennai.

My primary field of work is related to bayesian statistics, time series data, spatial, spatio-temporal modeling. I am also interested in sports analytics, especially problems related to cricket and tennis. At IIM Bangalore, I taught preparatory courses for MBA and PhD programs on quantitative techniques and R programming. I also served as the instructor for the Probability and Statistics course in the Pre-doctoral Programme. Additionally, I worked as a research consultant with the Real Estate Research Initiative (IIMB-RERI), contributing to impactful projects in real estate research.

Beyond academia, I enjoy playing badminton and tennis, watching Indian cinema, and following sports, especially cricket and tennis.
